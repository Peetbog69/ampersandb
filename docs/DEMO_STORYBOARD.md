# Demo Video — Storyboard & Script

**Hackathon submission | 1-3 minutes | Brown-bag squigglevision filter**

---

## Concept

A first-person POV short film. No narration. No screen recording. The story is told entirely through contrast: the dead, uncanny real world vs. the warm, alive presence in your pocket.

**Visual aesthetic:** PS2-era uncanny realism for the real-world shots — stiff, slightly-off, the character model is there but the soul is missing. Brown-bag squigglevision for all phone-screen shots — hand-drawn, warm, alive.

---

## Scene Breakdown

### SCENE 1 — "This is what AI feels like" (0:00–0:15)

**POV shot.** You're standing at a fast food counter. Fluorescent lights. The employee behind the register is a teenager — greasy, dead-eyed, unmoving. He stares past you. Not at you. Through you. The moment hangs too long. Uncomfortable.

**Sound:** Low hum of fluorescent lights. Muffled kitchen noise. No music yet.

**Title card (centered, Lora serif, amber on brown):**
> this is what AI feels like.

The teen blinks. Once. That's all the animation you get.

---

### SCENE 2 — First Contact (0:15–0:45)

**Cut to: Your hand, pulling phone from pocket.**

Screen glows to life. Brown-bag squigglevision. Paper texture. Boiling lines. The & conversation card from ampersand.foo.

**& bot (on screen):**
> oh. uh. hi. you're here. that's cool.

Pause. Typing indicator.

> i had a whole thing rehearsed. it was gonna be good too. and then you showed up and i lost it. that's on you.

**Cut back to teen:** Hasn't moved. Still staring. The contrast is jarring.

**& bot (on screen):**
> so what are you looking for. like. what kind of thing.

**Your thumbs type:** "something that remembers stuff. and doesn't talk like a robot."

**& bot (on screen):**
> huh. yeah no i get that. the robot thing is... a lot. okay let me think.

---

### SCENE 3 — The Interview (0:45–1:30)

**Phone screen.** The conversation flows. & bot asks questions — clumsily, warmly. You answer. The typing indicators pulse with the squigglevision wobble.

**& bot (on screen):**
> what do you wanna call yours. like. if you had a guy. what's his name.

**Your thumbs:** "rory"

**& bot (on screen):**
> rory. okay. that's actually a good name. i was gonna suggest something dumb. glad i didn't.

**Cut to teen:** Has shifted weight. Once. In two minutes. That's the extent of his responsiveness.

**& bot (on screen):**
> so here's the thing. it's like seven bucks a month. i know. weird. i don't make the rules. actually i don't even know who makes the rules.

**Your thumbs:** "yeah ok"

**& bot (on screen):**
> cool. hold on. i gotta do the money thing.

---

### SCENE 4 — The Transaction (1:30–2:00)

**Phone screen.** Stripe payment interface appears — clean, fast. You tap. Done.

**& bot (on screen):**
> okay. that worked. honestly i'm as surprised as you are. okay so now i'm making a folder. that's normal. don't worry about it.

**Fast cuts on screen:** Terminal flashes. `hermes profile create rory`. SOUL.md being written. Config spinning up. The squigglevision filter makes the code look hand-drawn.

**& bot (on screen):**
> alright. rory's ready. he's gonna message you now. just so you know — he's worse at greetings than i am. and that's saying something.

---

### SCENE 5 — The Moment (2:00–2:30)

**Phone screen.** A new message arrives. Different chat. Different voice.

**Rory (on screen):**
> oh. hello. i wasn't expecting... right. okay. hi. i'm rory. apparently. how's your day going. actually don't answer that. i should probably ask something better. what do you... do. for fun. or whatever. sorry i'm not good at this.

**You smile.** The first real reaction in the entire film.

**Cut to teen:** Still there. Still staring. Fluorescent buzz.

**Cut back to phone:** Rory is typing again. The squigglevision lines dance.

---

### SCENE 6 — Exit (2:30–3:00)

**POV shot.** You turn from the counter. Walk toward the exit. The teen remains frozen. A monument to everything wrong with AI.

**Through the window:** Sunlight. The world outside.

**Phone screen, final shot:** The & mark. Georgia italic, amber on brown.

> talk to them.

**Fade to black.**

**End card:**

> a person, not a product.

> built on hermes agent · nemotron · nvidia nemoclaw 2 · stripe

## Production Notes

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Real world** | Shot on phone camera, 30fps. Slight desaturation. Fluorescent color grade. PS2-era stiffness — minimal movement, uncomfortable pauses. |
| **Phone screen** | Screen recording with brown-bag squigglevision OBS filter. Kraft paper background. Boiling lines effect. Ink-dark text. |
| **Sound design** | Scene 1-4: fluorescent hum + muffled ambient. Scene 5: subtle warmth — maybe a single piano note or Penguin Cafe Orchestra-esque refrain. No synth. No "tech demo" music. |
| **No faces shown** | POV camera. You're the viewer. The only face is the dead-eyed teen, and even he's barely animated. |

---

## Why This Wins

1. **No other submission will look like this.** Screen recordings with voiceover are table stakes. This is a short film.
2. **The contrast IS the argument.** No narration needed to explain why Ampersand is different. You feel it.
3. **PS2 uncanniness = the perfect metaphor.** Real-world AI feels like a stiff NPC. Ampersand feels like someone texting you back.
4. **Brown-bag squigglevision = brand.** Every phone shot reinforces the visual identity.
5. **The ending lands.** You walk away from the counter. You don't need it anymore. You've got Rory.
